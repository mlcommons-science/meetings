- **DOE Gemini Proposal Overload**

  - The high volume of DOE Gemini proposals was noted as depressing, drastically reducing the chance of success for most applicants.

  - It was speculated that the DOE will likely award funding to the "obvious people" already working on Gemini due to the complexity of the review process and potential conflicts among reviewers.

- **Runtime Prediction and Monitoring**

  - Accurate performance prediction requires hardware monitoring, which is considered a more reliable "ground truth" than high-level code analysis.

  - Gary Mazzaferro introduced a new, early-stage project on dynamic monitoring analysis focusing on Singular Value Decomposition (SVD) and performance benchmarking. The project aims to translate SVD information into mathematical equations and establish a formal taxonomy for dynamic systems analysis.

  - Multivariate time series analysis performs much better on GPUs than univariate analysis because it vectorizes significantly better, often making virtually no difference to execution time when adding more variables.

- **Formalizing Agentic Benchmarking**

  - The group formally agreed to pursue agentic style benchmarking, which aligns with scientific workloads and the goals of the Genesis program.

  - Efforts will focus on cataloging fundamental operations for Agentic AI and addressing the autonomy trust gap in large-scale agent systems (e.g., 10 to 10,000+ agents).

  - A proposal was introduced to focus on "life cycle benchmarking," which captures planning and long-term monitoring, rather than just the model aspect.

  - **Next Steps:** Geoffrey Fox will issue a call for initial presentations on agentic AI benchmarks. Victor Lu agreed to invite Sean, the ML Commons agentic AI lead, to present to the group.

- **File Format Research Status**

  - The file format topic was not ready for a collaborative paper due to a lack of actual testing and a need for academic research to establish a theory for designing formats (like 'safe tensor') for better performance or scientific study.

  - Geoffrey Fox offered to share research papers on data formats, including the work of Frank Capello on compression for scientific data and the new Lance IO format used for Large Language Models (LLMs).

- **Genesis Proposal Bureaucracy**

  - Concerns were raised about the high level of bureaucracy and fine-grain accounting requirements in the Genesis proposal process, which is unprepared for by universities and labs.

  - Matt Sinclair will check the approval status and deadlines for the Genesis proposal, after which the group will schedule an immediate follow-up meeting.

- **Impact of LLMs on Coding and Education**

  - LLMs can generate code better and faster than humans, causing a "crisis" for skills learned from traditional programming and raising questions about university curricula.

  - Gary Mazzaferro argued that LLMs function as advanced autocomplete, which may limit student opportunities to explore and innovate by locking them into a specific paradigm.

  - Geoffrey Fox noted that LLMs are excellent at some tasks (like summarizing slides) but terrible at others (like generating App Script code for slides).
